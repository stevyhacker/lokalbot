# LokalBot Hero Demo — Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

Create a cinematic, native-Mac product film that feels precise, private, and alive. The app is the hero: real LokalBot captures stay legible inside a deep slate stage while the camera behaves like an attentive operator—decisive pushes, controlled focus pulls, and purposeful detail zooms. Motion should communicate interaction, not decoration. Teal is the signal for actions and recall; amber appears only for live/capture energy. The overall register is premium and warm, never cyberpunk or breathless.

## Colors

- `#080D14` — canvas; the darkest website slate, tinted blue rather than pure black.
- `#0F172A` — app plate and large structural panels.
- `#1E293B` — elevated slate for glass, captions, and keycaps.
- `#ECF3F7` — primary text; cool off-white.
- `#A2B4C2` — supporting text.
- `#23C4AE` — primary LokalBot teal for interaction.
- `#6EF2DC` — bright signal teal for active focus and confirmation.
- `#FBBF24` — amber, reserved for recording/live moments.

## Typography

- `SF Pro Display` — native product statements and scene titles, using 300 and 800/900 for strong contrast.
- `SF Mono` — captions, metadata, shortcuts, privacy proof, and interaction labels.
- Use tabular numerals for time and counters.

## Motion Language

- Camera moves have intent: push toward the exact UI being discussed, then hold long enough to read it.
- Primary transition: editorial push slide, 0.4–0.5 s, `power3.inOut`.
- Accents: zoom-through for the first app reveal; focus pull for the privacy/outro handoff.
- UI responses are snappy (0.16–0.28 s); camera moves are weightier (0.65–1.4 s).
- One ambient motion per scene: waveform, scanning light, timeline drift, or cursor breathing—not universal zoom.
- Cursor travel uses curved-looking two-axis timing, pauses over targets, then clicks with a restrained ring and sound.

## Audio Direction

- Warm, confident American-English narration with a conversational product-demo pace.
- Original low-volume ambient bed: soft slate-toned pad, muted pulse, no stock corporate ukulele.
- UI sound palette: soft ticks for typing, short filtered whooshes for scene pushes, restrained click transients, one luminous confirmation tone at the payoff.
- Narration always leads; music and effects duck beneath speech.

## What NOT to Do

- No slideshow labels pasted over static full-frame screenshots.
- No neon-purple or generic cyan cyberpunk treatment.
- No constant Ken Burns zoom, identical transitions, or repeated entrance motion.
- No fake claims, fake cloud dashboards, or invented app features.
- No tiny web typography, full-screen gradients, bouncy cursor motion, or loud game-like sound effects.
